In order to record your calorie intake you need to do a bit of ground work.

Suppose that you have just eaten a cheese sandwich. How do you record that? Well, you cant, at least not until you have done a little work first…

  1. Go to “Add to Calorie List”.

  2. In “item name” name the kind of bread that you use.

  3. In “calories per 100g/100ml” enter the number of calories.

  4. Press “Log it”.

Go through the same process for the cheese, butter, and whatever else you put on your sandwich.

Now that those basic foodstuffs are in your database, you can use them to make a cheese sandwich, (or anything else for that matter).

You need to combine these basic food stuffs into something that you eat.

  1. Go to “Create New Combo”.

  2. Enter a name for your combo, e.g. “Cheese Sandwich on wholemeal”.

  3. Click “Sumbit”.

  4. Go to “Edit Combo”.

  5. Select the combo you wish to edit.

  6. Click on the blue “+”.

  7. Select the basic foodstuff that you want to add, eg the bread.

  8. Input how many grammes of bread are in the sandwich.

  9. Click “Add to Combo”.

Repeat for all your other sandwich ingredients.

If you want to remove anything, then click on the “X” sign beside that item.

Your database now knows what is in your cheese sandwich, how much of it there is, and can calculate how many calories it contains.

To see what a combo item contains, go to “Display Combo”.

To see the calories in a combo item, go to “Display Combo Calories”.

Now you are ready to record that you have eaten a cheese sandwich.

  1. Go to “Log Your Intake”.

  2. Select your combo.

  3. Change the date and time if the default does not suit

  4. press “Log it!”.

To view your intake, go to “Display Intake”.

This shows a list of your total calories per day.

Click on a day, to see a break down of your intake for that day. Click on it again to collapse it.
